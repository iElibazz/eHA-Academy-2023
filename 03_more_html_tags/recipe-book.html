Jollof Rice

I can‚Äôt think of a more popular West African dish than Jollof rice. It‚Äôs popular for good reason ‚Äî it is delicious!

Ingredients

Item Details
Rice The main ingredient in jollof rice is, of course, rice.
Flavor and texture Canned tomato, fresh tomatoes, bell peppers, scotch bonnet, onions, garlic, and ginger
Oil You‚Äôll also need oil for frying your sauce‚Äîany kind of vegetable oil will do.
Stock Chicken, beef, or vegetable stock are great choices here for adding depth of flavor.
Seasoning and herbs Curry powder, salt, black/white pepper, bouillon powder, thyme, and bay leaves.

How to prepare

  1. The inevitable foundation for building up the flavor in this meal is to start by sauteeing the Onions. This should take about 3 to 5 minutes. The next thing is to stir in the tomato paste. This adds a deep and rich tomato flavor; I simply fry this for another 5 minutes or thereabout.
  2. Add the ginger and garlic and cook along with the tomato paste for another two minutes; Actually, by the time this is ready, you will notice it in the smell. If you keep it cooking any longer, you might risk burning them.
  3. Add the blended peppers. Usually, there is no particular time frame for frying this pepper. I often stop when the sauce becomes really thick, and the oil literally floats on top of the sauce. At this point, most of the water is gone, and the sauce no longer smells raw. Trust me, you will know, but just in case you are still in doubt, I would say maybe 15 to 20 minutes depending on the quantity of water in your blended pepper.
  4. Finally, in building my flavor, I add the thyme, curry powder, salt, white pepper (good but optional), and seasoning cubes. Adjust the seasoning at this point if there is a need to.
  5. Once the flavor is on point, then, I stir in the Rice. Make sure you stir the rice properly until you cover each grain of rice with the sauce.
  6. Now add the chicken stock. Give it a brief stir and cover it up with a tight-fitting lid. If your lid is not fitting enough, simply cover the rice with foil paper before covering it with the lid. This is because Jollof needs a lot of steam in order to turn out well.
  7. Once the rice comes to a boil, reduce the heat to medium-low immediately and continue to cook until the rice is done, about 20 to 30 minutes.
  8. Serve with Fried Chicken, Spicy Grilled Chicken, Grilled Tilapia Fish, or Gizzards and plantains.

Meat Pie

West African Meat pie or the Nigerian meat pie is light and slightly Flaky and it‚Äôs stuffed with well seasoned Ground Beef (minced Beef), potatoes, and carrots. These Meat pies are a comfort to the soul and a delight to the palate. They can be eaten as breakfast, brunch, or as a snack.

Ingredients

Item Details
Flour I used unbleached all-purpose flour. Feel free to use any brand of flour you want.
Butter/Margarine I used butter and Margarine in a 50:50 ratio. I love the taste and texture the butter adds to my pies.
Salt and Sugar I have always used only salt for the longest time. Now, I use both salt and sugar.
Meat pie filling Carrots, Potatoes, Salt, Curry powder, Thyme, Bouillon Cubes, Water, Flour, Ground beef, vegetable Oil.

How to prepare

  • Meat Pie Filling
    1. Add the oil to a pan and saute the onions for about 1 minute. Add the ground beef and cook until it's well browned.
    2. Add the curry powder, thyme, bouillon powder (cube), and salt and mix together. Then stir in the carrots and potatoes. Cook till tender.
    3. In a separate bowl, mix the flour and water until smooth and stir the mixture with the filling (this will thicken the filling).
    4. Remove from heat and allow to cool
  • Meat Pie Pastry
    1. Combine the flour, baking powder, and nutmeg in a bowl. Mix well
    2. Add the Margarine and butter (or margarine alone if you choose to) and rub it with the flour until you get a crumbly texture ‚Äì like Breadcrumbs then begin to add Cold Water, a little at a time until it forms a stiff dough
    3. Transfer the dough to a work surface and knead for 1 minute then place the dough back in the bowl, cover it with a plastic wrap, and place it in the fridge to rest for about 30 minutes ( skip refrigerating if you want a less flaky pie).
  • Let‚Äôs put them together
    1. Roll out the dough to about 1/4 of an inch thick then cut out your desired size circles and distribute the filling inside each cut-out pie dough (don't overfill so you can be able to easily close the Pie).
    2. Crack an egg in a small bowl, add 1 tablespoon of water and whisk together then use a pastry brush to brush the edges of the Pie
    3. Fold the pie over to seal (and crimp the edge if not using a pie cutter) and use a fork to create a couple of vents on the Pie (this will help prevent air pockets in your pie)
    4. Brush the surface of the Pie with the Egg wash and arrange the pies on a parchment paper-lined tray.
    5. Bake at 350¬∞F for 20 to 30 minutes or till golden brown.
    6. There you have it! Your fresh Nigerian meat pie is all done!!. Serve with a cup of hot Tea for morning breakfast or a chill drink for afternoon or evening snack. Enjoy!

Egusi Soup

Egusi is a traditional one-pot soup that combines blended melon seed, pepper, leafy vegetables, and meat. It is nutty, spicy, and tasty.

Ingredients

Item Details
Egusi melon This is the main ingredient you need to make this soup.
Palm Oil used to cook the pepper mix as a base before adding the egusi.
Beef, dried fish, and Crayfish these are my protein sources for a hearty soup.
Locust beans adds an earthy umami taste to the recipe.
Spinach I used spinach in this recipe, but you can use other leafy green vegetables
Pepper mix my blended pepper mix combines red bell pepper, habanero pepper, Fresno pepper, and onions.
Onions You still need to define the soup‚Äôs aroma in addition to the onion in the pepper mix.
Stock Cube this is optional but a worthwhile seasoning addition.
Salt Of course, you need salt to taste.
Beef stock this will enhance the flavor. Chicken stock works just as well too.

How to prepare

  1. Blend the peppers and onions together
  2. Blend the Egusi melon
  3. Heat the palm oil but don‚Äôt overheat
  4. Add onions and locust bean
  5. Add the blended pepper
  6. Add the remaining locust bean, beef stock, and salt to taste. Cover and leave to cook for another 5 minutes
  7. Gently introduce the blended egusi inside the sauce but don‚Äôt stir it at this point. Just cover it and leave it to cook for about 20 to 30 minutes. Keep an eye on it, so it doesn‚Äôt burn but try not to stir it until the Egusi turns into a soft compact mass. Then, gently stir together and add more stock or water if necessary.
  8. Add the fish and meat and cook for another 5 minutes.
  9. Stir in the spinach and leave to simmer for about 5 minutes (stir constantly). Adjust the seasoning if necessary.
  10. Serve while still hot.
